Adapted Physical Education (APE)

Adapted Physical Education (APE) is specifically modified or adapted physical education to serve students with disabilities. Physical Education for students with disabilities is defined as the development of physical and motor skills, fundamental motor skills and patterns, and skills in aquatics, dance, and individual and group games and sports. APE teachers provide a variety of services including planning, assessments of abilities and environments, evaluation of services, and advocating for students. Because physical education for students with disabilities is a federally mandated component of special education services, APE is a direct service. APE may be provided in a variety of settings such as General PE, small-group, one-on-one or hospital homebound as necessary. Teacher qualifications include Health and PE certification, and Special Education certification.

Teachers and paraprofessionals working in the ACHIEVE Program can utilize the website to facilitate physical activities adapted to fit their students’ unique needs. Students within the program receive 1 hour of service per week, however, many teachers like to keep the same daily schedule. This website will allow teachers to see what we are working on so they can reiterate movements, and terminology and give the students an opportunity for purposeful play more than their “allotted” time. Students and their families can utilize the website to participate in physical activities adapted to fit their students’ unique needs. Adapted physical education is meant to provide students with lifelong skills and healthy habits. This website allows students and their families to continue creating healthy habits outside of the classroom.
